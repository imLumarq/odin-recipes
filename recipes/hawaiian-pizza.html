Hawaiian Pizza

Description

Typically topped with tomato sauce, cheddar cheese, sliced ham, and pineapple.

The combination of savory and fruity toppings smell delicious from the oven and might eben convert a few anti-pineapple family members.

If you don't like pineapples on your pizza, than we are not friends.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for pan
  • 2 slices applewood-smoked bacon
  • 4 canned pineapple rings
  • 500 grams fresh pizza dough, at room temperature
  • ½ cup pizza sauce
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • ¼ cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 4 slices of bacon, but into cubes (this ingredient is optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 260 degrees C (500 degrees F) with a rack in the lower position. Oil a large rimmed baking sheet with olive oil.
  2. Meanwhile, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add bacon and cook, flipping occasionally, until golden brown and crisp, 5 to 7 minutes. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate.
  3. Pour out pan drippings without wiping pan clean and return skillet to hear. Add pineapple, cook until lightly browned and caramelized, 3 minutes per side. Remove from pan, Chop bacon and cut pineapple rings into 9mm pieces.
  4. On a lightly floured surface, roll dough to a 31cm circle and place on the oiled baking sheet. Prick dough with a fork several times to reduce air bubbles. Spread pizza sauce evenly over dough Sprinkle with cheese, red onion, chopped pineapple and chopped bacon.
  5. Bake in preheated oven until cheese is melted and bubbly, toppings are lightly charred and crust is golden brown, 10 to 15 minutes. Let stand 5 minutes before serving.

Cook's Note

You can always sprinkle as many pineapple has you like, more always means better :).
