Lasagna

Description

It's one of the ultimate one-pot meals, whether you're cooking for a potluck or just the family dinner, it's hard to go wrong with lasagna.

Layers of sauce, cheese and meat, sandwiched between sheets of pasta, make for a dish that's just as fun to eat as it is to make.

Diet food this is not.

Ingredients

  • 4 zucchini
  • 1 ½ cups homemade or store-bought tomato sauce
  • ⅔ cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmigiano Reggiano cheese
  • 1 ½ cups bechamel sauce
  • ¼ cup fresh basil, chopped

Steps

  1. Preheat oven ot 190 degrees C (375 degrees F)
  2. Cut zucchini lengthwise into 6.35mm thick slices with a knife or mandolin
  3. Pour 2 tablespoons tomato sauce on the bottom of a 22.86 x 33.02 cm baking dish
  4. Top with a thin layer of mozzarella, 1/3 of the bechamel (see Editor's Note), 1/3 of remaining tomato sauce, 1/3 of the Parmigiano Reggiano cheese and 1/3 of the basil. Repeat layers, topping with bechamel and Parmigiano Reggiano cheese
  5. Bake in preheated oven until sauce is bubbly and the top is golden brow, about 35 minutes, Allow to set until remaining liquid is absorbed, about 10 minutes

Cook's Note

Use a meat ragu instead of marinara sauce if you prefer
